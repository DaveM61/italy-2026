Detailed Breakdown

Pros & Cons

✈ Option A — Fly
BRI → Rome FCO → Florence FLR  |  11:20 AM departure
Pros
  • Earlier Arezzo arrival (~4:45 PM) — time to settle before dinner
  • Airport car rental: on-site, no ZTL exposure in Florence
  • Cleaner morning: full group stays together at BRI
  • Later villa departure — 8:30 AM is a civilized start
Cons
  • Rome FCO layover is 1 hr 20 min — workable, but a first-leg delay misses the connection
  • More expensive: ~$95/person more than the train
  • Economy fare likely restricted — no changes, no refunds; verify before booking
  • Baggage check-in and security for the full group
🚄 Option B — Train
Frecciarossa 8818, Bari → Florence SMN  |  9:30 AM departure
Pros
  • Saves ~$660 for the group (~$95/person less)
  • No connection to miss — a delay doesn't strand anyone
  • No baggage claim, no security lines — board and go
  • Frecciarossa is a scenic, comfortable ride through southern Italy
Cons
  • 6:45 AM villa departure — early morning for the group
  • Split front-end: 2 drivers must return cars to BRI and taxi back (~45 min round trip)
  • Later Arezzo arrival (~6:30 PM) — less time before dinner
  • ZTL risk at Florence SMN car rental pickup — requires careful navigation
Recommendation

Bottom Line

Option A (Fly) is the smoother travel day — later villa departure, cleaner car rental logistics, and 90 minutes more time in Arezzo before dinner. The Rome connection at 1h 20m is the one real risk; it's workable but worth knowing. The cost premium is modest at ~$95/person.

Option B (Train) saves meaningful money and eliminates connection anxiety, but asks for an early morning and delivers you to Arezzo later in the evening.
Dave's lean: Option A — the logistics and earlier arrival are worth the cost difference for this group. But happy to be outvoted. Reply with your preference.
